Organizations are heavily investing in AI tools, expecting productivity gains to translate into faster, more reliable software delivery. Yet only 1% of companies consider themselves "mature" on the deployment spectrum, and research consistently shows that most are struggling to scale AI beyond individual productivity gains or isolated wins. The companies that are seeing real returns share some common characteristics, which focus on the systems already in place, like the underlying automated infrastructure built before AI tools came into play. 

The research explores why individual AI productivity gains so often fail to translate into organizational gains, and where the real constraints lie. AI excels at high-volume routine tasks, but developers would much rather help with Continuous Delivery practices like deployment automation, security, compliance, onboarding, and incident management. 

In this webinar, the Octopus Deploy Developer Relations and Research team will examine the automation gap holding back their AI investment and what to do about it.

We'll cover:

- How unchanged pipelines absorb the throughput gains AI creates
- Why AI-generated code amplifies review and integration bottlenecks specifically
- Which Continuous Delivery capabilities do high-performing teams build before scaling AI
- How to align engineering and platform teams around delivery infrastructure as a strategic priority

Automation through Continuous Delivery practices (such as CI automation, automated testing, deployment automation, and observability) addresses the bottlenecks that absorb individual gains before they reach production. Still, they require deliberate investment before AI can deliver value.

The research is consistent: organizations that see returns from their AI investments build their foundations first, and without this, AI rarely delivers the improvements teams are hoping for.
